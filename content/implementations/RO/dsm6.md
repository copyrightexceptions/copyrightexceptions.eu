description: "This exception allows for the making of copies of protected works or objects in the permanent collections of cultural heritage conservation institutions, in any format or on any type of support, for the purpose of preservation and to the extent necessary for such preservation. In addition libraries, museums, film libraries, sound libraries, archives of public cultural or scientific institutions, which operate on a non-profit basis can reproduce (i) short extracts from works for information and research, (ii) a complete work, for its replacement, in case of destruction, serious damage or loss of the single copy from the permanent collection of the respective library or archive (art.35.1.d), and (iii) specific reproductions made by publicly accessible libraries, educational institutions or museums or by archives, which are not made for the purpose of obtaining a commercial or economic advantage, directly or indirectly (art.35.1.e)."

- "3-step test: the exception only applies in special cases, provided that they do not conflict with the normal exploitation of the work or other protected subject matter and do not prejudice the author or the rights holders (art. 36^5(2))"
remarks: "The transposition of art.6 of the CDSM Directive was introduced in 2022, existing in parallel and partially overlapping with the pre-existing implementation of Article 5(2)(c) of the InfoSoc Directive in art.35(1)(d) and (e) of the law.<br /><br />According to art. 36^5, any contractual provision that does not comply with the exception is not enforceable. Furthermore, rightholders who have established technical protection measures are obliged to make available to the beneficiaries the means necessary for legal access to works or any other subject matter of protection. However, 'rightholders may limit the number of copies made' (art. 36^5(4)). Although Romania has a broad implementation of art. 6 of the CDSM Directive, the unusual possibility for rightholders to limit the number of copies made under the exception compromise its imperative nature and warrant a lower score. However, it should be also acknowledged that the provision of art. 36^4 coexist with a very broad and technology neutral reproduction exception in art.35(e) which does not contain a restriction in terms of the number of copies made and may cover cases that fall outside of art. 36^4.<br /><br/>According to art. 120, the provisions regarding the limits of exercising copyright also apply accordingly to related rights. Under art. 134 they apply by analogy to broadcasting organisations. Art. 94^1 makes all exceptions applicable to press publications. As per art. 36^4(1), this particular exception also applies to sui generis database rights."
