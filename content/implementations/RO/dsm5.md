description: "This exception allows for communication to the public and making available to the public, for digital uses of works and other protected subject matter, including in the online and cross-border environment, to the extent justified by the non-commercial purpose pursued, by the sole objective of didactic illustration, provided that such use: (i) takes place under the responsibility of an educational institution, in its building or in another place, or in a secure electronic environment to which only the pupils or students and teaching staff of the educational institution have access to, and (ii) does not replace or affect the purchase of materials intended for the educational market. Article 36^3 coexist with a very broad and technology neutral general illustration for teaching and research exception in Article 35(d) which may cover cases that fall outside the implementation of Arcticle 5 of the CDSM Directive." 
beneficiaries:
- any user (but under the responsibility of an educational institution, in its building or in another place, or in a secure electronic environment to which only the pupils or students and teaching staff of the educational institution have access to)
purposes: 
- non-commercial
- didactic illustration
usage:
-  communication to the public
-  making available to the public
-  reproductions, adaptation and distribution (of computer programs)
-  extraction and reuse (of sui generis databases)
subjectmatter:
- works
- performances
- phonograms
- film fixations
- broadcasts
- press publications
- databases
compensation:
- no compensation required
attribution: 
- use must be accompanied by an indication of the source, including the name of the author, except in cases where this proves impossible
otherConditions: 
- use must be limited to the extent justified by the non-commercial purpose pursued
- use must not replace or affect the purchase of materials intended for the educational market
- rightholders may limit the number of copies made (art. 36^5(4))
- "3-step test: the exception only applies in special cases, provided that they do not conflict with the normal exploitation of the work or other protected subject matter and do not prejudice the author or the rights holders (art. 36^5(2))"
- if the educational institution is under the jurisdiction of Romania, it is considered that the use takes place only on the national territory
remarks: "According to art. 36^5, any contractual provision that does not comply with the exception is unenforceable. Furthermore, rightholders who have established technical protection measures are obliged to make available to beneficiaries the means necessary for lawful access to works or other protected subject matter. However, use must not replace or affect the purchase of materials intended for the educational market, and 'rightholders may limit the number of copies made' (art. 36^5(4)). Although Romania has a relatively broad implementation of art. 5 of the CDSM Directive, the unclear conditions of the carve-out regarding educational materials (use must not replace or affect the purchase of materials intended for the educational market) and the unusual possibility for rightholders to limit the number of copies made under the exception compromise its imperative nature and warrant a low score. However, it should be also acknowledged that the provision of art. 36^3 coexist with a very broad and technology neutral general illustration for teaching and research exception in art.35(d) which may cover cases that fall outside the implementation in art. 36^3. <br /><br/>According to art. 120 of the Romanian Law No 8/1996 on copyright and related rights, the provisions regarding the limits of copyright also apply accordingly to related rights. Under art. 134 they apply by analogy to broadcasting organisations as well. Art. 94^1 makes all exceptions applicable to press publications. As per art. 36^1(2), this particular exception also applies to sui generis database rights."
