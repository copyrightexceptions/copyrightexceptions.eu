description: "These exceptions allow for the reproduction within libraries, museums, film libraries, sound libraries, archives of public cultural or scientific institutions, which operate on a non-profit basis i) of short extracts from works for information and research; ii) of a complete work, for its replacement, in case of destruction, serious damage or loss of the single copy from the permanent collection of the respective library or archive (art.35.1.d); iii) specific reproductions made by publicly accessible libraries, educational institutions or museums or by archives, which are not made for the purpose of obtaining a commercial or economic advantage, directly or indirectly (art.35.1.e). In addition, as of 2022, the implementation of Article 6 of the CDSM Directive in Article 36^4 allows for the making of copies of protected works or objects in the permanent collections of cultural heritage conservation institutions, in any format or on any type of support, for the purpose of preservation and to the extent necessary for such preservation."

otherConditions:
- reproduction must cover short extracts from works (for information and research)
- reproduction of an entire work is permitted only for its replacement, in case of destruction, serious damage or loss of the single copy from the permanent collection of the respective library or archive
- works used must be in the permanent collections of cultural heritage conservation institutions (for preservation purposes)
- use must be effected to the extent necessary for such preservation (for preservation purposes)
- rightholders may limit the number of copies made (for preservation purposes - art. 36^5(4))
- "3-step test: the exception only applies in special cases, provided that they do not conflict with the normal exploitation of the work or other protected subject matter and do not prejudice the author or the rights holders (art. 36^5(2))"
remarks: "Romania has a very broad and technology neutral reproduction exception in art.35(e), which is a verbatim transposition of art.5.2(c) of the InfoSoc Directive. However, the parallel existance of three separate normative texts covering reproduction by CHIs and having different scopes and conditions, with no clear collision rules, may lead to legal uncertainty.<br /><br />According to some commentators, art.35.1(d) is the implementation of art. 5.3(n) of the InfoSoc Directive. However, its first case concerning uses for the purposes of information and research only covers reproduction of short excerpts and does not mention in any way providing access to the public. It is not entirely clear what the relationship between the second sentence of art. 35.1(d) and art.35.1.(e) is. The latter is clearly a literal implementation of art.5.2(c) of the InfoSoc Directive, while the former represents an extremely restrictive approach to reproduction for preservation purposes by cultural heritage institutions. There is no known judicial interpretation available. In addition, the transposition of art.6 of the CDSM Directive introduced in 2022, exists in parallel and partially overlaps with the pre-existing arts.35(1)(d) and (e) of the law. According to art. 36^5, any contractual provision that does not comply with this particular exception is not enforceable. Furthermore, rightholders who have established technical protection measures are obliged to make available to the beneficiaries the means necessary for legal access to works or any other subject matter of protection. However, 'rightholders may limit the number of copies made' (art. 36^5(4)). This unusual possibility for rightholders to limit the number of copies made under the exception compromise its imperative nature. It should be acknowledged that the provision of art. 36^4 coexist with the broad and technology neutral reproduction exception in art.35(e) which does not contain a restriction in terms of the number of copies made and may cover cases that fall outside of art. 36^4. On the other hand, art.35(e) is not protected against contractual override. These complexities and lack of clarity on the scope of the permitted reproduction justify a lower score, notwithstanding the broad reach of art.35(e).<br /><br />According to art. 120, the provisions regarding the limits of exercising copyright also apply accordingly to related rights. Under art. 134 they apply by analogy to broadcasting organizations. Art. 94^1 makes all exceptions applicable to press publications. As per art. 36^4(1), the preservation exception also applies to sui generis database rights."
