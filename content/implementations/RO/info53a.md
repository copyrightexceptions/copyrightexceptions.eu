description: "The exception pursuant to Article 35(d) allows for the use of disclosed works for the sole purpose of didactic illustration or scientific research, provided that such use complies with fair practice, does not conflict with the normal exploitation of the work and does not prejudice the author or the holders of the rights of use. In addition, Articles 36^3 covers communication to the public and making available to the public for digital uses of works and other protected subject matter, including in the online and cross-border environment, to the extent justified by the non-commercial purpose pursued, by the sole objective of didactic illustration, provided that such use: (i) takes place under the responsibility of an educational institution, in its building or in another place, or in a secure electronic environment to which only the pupils or students and teaching staff of the educational institution have access to, and (ii) does not replace or affect the purchase of materials intended for the educational market. Article 36^3 coexist with a very broad and technology neutral general illustration for teaching and research exception in Article 35(d) which may cover cases that fall outside the implementation of Arcticle 5 of the CDSM Directive."

compensation:
- no compensation required
attribution: 
- the source and the name of the author must be mentioned, unless this proves impossible; in the case of works of fine art, photography or architecture, the place where the original is located must also be mentioned (art. 35(d))
- use must be accompanied by an indication of the source, including the name of the author, except in cases where this proves impossible (art. 36^3)
otherConditions: 
- works used must be disclosed (art. 35(d))
- use must comply with fair practice (art. 35(d))
- use must be limited to the extent justified by the non-commercial purpose pursued (art. 36^3)
- use must be effected under the responsibility of an educational institution, in its building or in another place, or in a secure electronic environment to which only the pupils or students and teaching staff of the educational institution have access to (art. 36^3)
- use must not replace or affect the purchase of materials intended for the educational market (art. 36^3)
- rightholders may limit the number of copies made (art. 36^3)
- "3-step test: the exception only applies in special cases, provided that they do not conflict with the normal exploitation of the work or other protected subject matter and do not prejudice the author or the rightsholders"
- if the educational institution is under the jurisdiction of Romania, it is considered that the use takes place only on the national territory (art. 36^3)
remarks: "In Romania, a very broad and technology neutral implementation of art. 5(3)(a) of the InfoSoc Directive in art.35(d) of the Copyright Act coexists with a relatively limited implementation of art.5 of the CDSM Directive, without clear collision rules to determine the scope of permitted educational uses.<br /><br/>According to art. 36^5, any contractual provision that does not comply with the digital use exception is unenforceable. Furthermore, rightholders who have established technical protection measures are obliged to make available to beneficiaries the means necessary for lawful access to works or other protected subject matter. However, under art. 36^3, use must not replace or affect the purchase of materials intended for the educational market, and 'rightholders may limit the number of copies made' (art. 36^5(4)). Although Romania has a relatively broad implementation of art. 5 of the CDSM Directive, the unclear conditions of the carve-out regarding educational materials (use must not replace or affect the purchase of materials intended for the educational market) and the unusual possibility for rightholders to limit the number of copies made under the exception compromise its imperative nature.<br /><br/>According to art. 120 of the Romanian Law No 8/1996 on copyright and related rights, the provisions regarding the limits of copyright also apply accordingly to related rights. Under art. 134 they apply by analogy to broadcasting organisations as well. Art. 94^1 makes all exceptions applicable to press publications. As per art. 36^1(2), the digital use exception also applies to sui generis database rights."
