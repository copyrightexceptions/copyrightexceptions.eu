description: "This exception allows for the reproduction and communication to the public of works for the exclusive purpose of illustrating teaching or scientific research to the extent justified by the non-commercial purpose pursued, and provided that such use is in accordance with good practices. In addition, a new art. 10, 2bis° covers digital use of works, for the exclusive purpose of illustration in the context of teaching, to the extent justified by the non-commercial purpose pursued, provided that this usetakes place under the responsibility of an educational institution, on its premises or elsewhere, or through a secure electronic environment accessible only to the pupils, students, and teaching staff of that institution." 
beneficiaries:
- any user
purposes: 
- illustration in the context of teaching
- research
- non-commercial
usage:
- reproduction and communication to the public (general clause)
- any use (digital teaching)
subjectmatter:
- works (incl. computer programmes and databases)
- performances
- phonograms
- film fixations
- broadcasts
- databases (art. 10, 2bis°)
compensation:
- no compensation required
attribution: 
- the source, including the name of the author, must be indicated, unless this proves impossible
otherConditions: 
- use must be limited to the extent justified by the non-commercial purpose pursued
- use must be in accordance with good practices
- in the case of digital teaching, use must take place under the responsibility of an educational institution, on its premises or elsewhere or through a secure electronic environment accessible only to the pupils, students, and teaching staff of that institution
- digital teaching carried out using secure electronic environments is deemed to take place only in the Member State of the European Union in which the educational establishment is located
remarks: "Both the implementation of art. 5(3)(a) of the InfoSoc Directive and the one of art.5 of the CDSM Directive in Luxembourg are very broad and closely resemble the EU clauses. In 2022, the national legislator added a general rule in a new paragraph 4 of art.10, according to which all exceptions are imperative (non-overriddable by contract).<br /><br/>The scope of both exceptions is extended to neighbouring rights under art. 46 (1), 9° and art. 46 (2) - for performers, phonogram and film producers; art. 55 refers to art. 46 for broadcasters and art.56bis(3) - for ress publishers. The exception under art. 10, 2bis° also covers the sui generis database right (art. 68(2)). All exceptions apply mutatis mutandis to the distribution right."
