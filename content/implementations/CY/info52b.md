title: "Section 7(2)(o) of the Copyright Law"
date: 2004
draft: false
weight: 41
exceptions:
- info52b
jurisdictions:
- CY
score: 3
description: "This exception allows for the reproduction on any medium carried out by a natural person for private use and for non-direct or indirect commercial purposes, provided that the rightholders receive fair compensation that takes into account the application or not of technological measures to the specific work or other material" 
beneficiaries:
- natural persons
purposes: 
- private use
- non-commercial
usage:
- reproduction (on any medium)
subjectmatter:
- works (incl. computer programmes)
- databases
- phonograms
- film fixations
- broadcasts
- publications of previously unpublished works
- performances
- press publications
compensation:
- fair compensation required (that takes into account the application or not of technological measures to the specific work or other material)
attribution: 
- no attribution required
otherConditions: 
remarks: "In terms of subject matter, Cyprus' exceptions apply to 'intellectual property'. According to Section 3(1)(a) of the Law, the material scope of protection of 'intellectual property' includes works, incl. computer programmes, but also databases, phonograms, broadcasts and publications of previously unpublished works. Furthermore, Section 3(1)(c)(iii) of the Law states that the exceptions and limitations recognized in art. 3 and in art. 7 vis-a-vis 'intellectual property' also apply to related rights, in the absence of a contrary provision. According to Section 3(1)(b) the objects of related rights are press publications and performances."
