description: "This exception allows for the use of a work made for the purposes of judicial, parliamentary or administrative proceedings or reference to any such proceedings." 
beneficiaries:
- any user
purposes: 
- judicial, parliamentary or administrative proceedings 
- reference to any such proceedings
usage:
- any use
subjectmatter:
- works (incl. computer programmes)
- databases
- phonograms
- film fixations
- broadcasts
- publications of previously unpublished works
- performances
- press publications
compensation:
- no compensation required
attribution: 
- no attribution required
otherConditions: 
remarks: "Although broad and technology neutral, the Cypriot implementation of art. 5(c)(e) of the InfoSoc Directive only covers one of the two purposes of use, ommiting the public security purpose, contained in the EU clause. In contrast, Section 7C(3)(b)(iii)(c), implementing art. 9(c) of the Database Directive, includes extraction and/or reuse for both reasons of public safety and administrative or judicial proceedings.<br /><br/>In terms of subject matter, Cyprus' exceptions apply to 'intellectual property'. According to Section 3(1)(a) of the Law, the material scope of protection of 'intellectual property' includes works, incl. computer programmes, but also databases, phonograms, broadcasts and publications of previously unpublished works. Furthermore, Section 3(1)(c)(iii) of the Law states that the exceptions and limitations recognized in art. 3 and in art. 7 vis-a-vis 'intellectual property' also apply to related rights, in the absence of a contrary provision. According to Section 3(1)(b) the objects of related rights are press publications and performances."
