title: "Section 27 of the Copyright Law"
date: 2022
draft: false
weight: 13
exceptions:
- dsm6
jurisdictions:
- CY
score: 2
description: "This exception allows cultural heritage institutions to create copies of a work or other subject-matter permanently in their collections, in any form and by any means, for the purposes of preserving the said works or other subject-matter and to the extent required for such preservation."
beneficiaries:
- cultural heritage institutions
purposes: 
- preservation
usage:
- reproduction (in any form and by any means)
- making available to the public
- reproduction (in whole or in part), translation, adaptation, arrangement, presentation, communication or display to the public (of databases)
subjectmatter:
- works (incl. computer programmes)
- databases
- phonograms
- film fixations
- broadcasts
- publications of previously unpublished works
- performances
- press publications
compensation:
- no compensation required
attribution: 
- no attribution required
otherConditions: 
- works used must be permanently in beneficiaries' permanent collections
- use must be effected to the extent required for preservation
- "3-step test: the exception applies only in special cases which do not conflict with the normal exploitation of the work or other protected subject-matter and do not unreasonably prejudice the legitimate interests of the rightholder"
- contractual arrangements contrary to the exception are unenforceable (§28(1), §44(1))
remarks: "In terms of subject matter, this exception covers 'works and other subject matter'. In addition, according to §44(3), 'the provisions of arts 24 to 43 shall apply to all works and other subject matter protected by national copyright law on or after the date of entry into force of the provisions of this Law.' The approach to the implementation of the CDSM Directive differs from the pre-existing one, where there is one main exception applying to 'intellectual property' (meaning works, incl. computer programmes, but also databases, phonograms, broadcasts and publications of previously unpublished works) and a reference to related rights (meaning press publications and performances).<br /><br/>In terms of types of usage, the exception mentions making of copies, but explicitly covers reproduction, but also making available to the public, as well as translation, adaptation, arrangement, presentation, communication or display to the public of the results of databases.<br /><br/>The second paragraph of the provision states that acts of reproduction carried out by cultural heritage institutions or organizations for purposes other than the preservation of works and other protected subject-matter in their permanent collections shall be subject to the authorization of the rightholders, 'unless such acts may be carried out pursuant to other exceptions or limitations provided for in European Union law.' On the other hand, the Cypriot implementation of art. 5(2)(c) of the InfoSoc Directive was amended to include that 'the application of the exception is subject to the exceptions and limitations laid down in Articles 24 to 44,' which suggests that the exceptions introduced pursuant to arts 3–8 of the CDSM Directive take precedence over the pre-existing provisions, while the implementation of art. 5(2)(c) can serve as a fall-back exception.<br /><br/>The exception is safeguarded against contractual override (§28(1), §44(1)). However, it does not appear to be protected against circumvention via TPMs. Since many pre-existing exceptions are protected against TPMs under §14B(3) and (4), this is most likely an omission on the part of the legislator during the implementation of Article 7(2) of the CDSM Directive. This omission, however, warrants a lower score for the exception."
