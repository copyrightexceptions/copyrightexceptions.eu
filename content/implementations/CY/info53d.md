title: "Section 7(2)(f) of the Copyright Law"
date: 2004
draft: true
weight: 54
exceptions:
- info53d
jurisdictions:
- CY
score: 2
description: "This exception allows for the quotation of excerpts for the purpose of criticism or book presentation, provided that they concern a work or other objects which have already been lawfully made available to the public, and that such quotation is in accordance with good morals and its extent is justified by its purpose." 
beneficiaries:
- any user
purposes: 
- criticism 
- book presentation
usage:
- any use (quotation)
subjectmatter:
- works (excerpts)
- databases (excerpts)
- phonograms (excerpts)
- film fixations (excerpts)
- broadcasts (excerpts)
- publications of previously unpublished works (excerpts)
- performances (excerpts)
- press publications (excerpts)
compensation:
- no compensation required
attribution: 
- the source, including the name of the author, must be mentioned unless it is established that this is impossible
otherConditions: 
- works used must have already been lawfully made available to the public
- use is limited to excerpts
- the quotation must be in accordance with good morals 
- the extent of the use must be justified by its purpose
remarks: "The Cyprus' quotation exception is limited to excerpts only. In terms of subject matter, Cyprus' exceptions apply to 'intellectual property'. According to Section 3(1)(a) of the Law, the material scope of protection of 'intellectual property' includes works, incl. computer programmes, but also databases, phonograms, broadcasts and publications of previously unpublished works. Furthermore, Section 3(1)(c)(iii) of the Law states that the exceptions and limitations recognized in art. 3 and in art. 7 vis-a-vis 'intellectual property' also apply to related rights, in the absence of a contrary provision. According to Section 3(1)(b) the objects of related rights are press publications and performances."
