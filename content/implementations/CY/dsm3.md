description: "This exception allows for the reproduction and extraction by research organisations and cultural heritage institutions, for the purpose of carrying out, for purposes of scientific research, text and data mining from works or other subject matter to which beneficiaries have lawful access." 
beneficiaries:
- research organisations 
- cultural heritage institutions
purposes: 
- scientific research
usage:
- reproduction (of 'intellectual property' other than computer programmes, and related rights, other than press publications)
- making available to the public (of 'intellectual property' and related rights, other than press publications)
- reproduction (in whole or in part) (of copyrighted databases)
- extraction and re-use (of sui generis right databases)
subjectmatter:
- works (other than computer programmes)
- databases
- phonograms
- film fixations
- broadcasts
- publications of previously unpublished works
- performances
- press publications (unclear)
compensation:
- no compensation required
attribution: 
- no attribution required
otherConditions: 
- lawful access
- "storage and reuse: copies of works or other protected subject matter must be stored with an appropriate level of security and may be kept for the purposes of scientific research, including the verification of the results of the research"
- "safeguards: rightsholders are permitted to implement measures aimed at ensuring the security and integrity of the networks and databases where the works or other protected subject matter are hosted. Such measures must not exceed what is necessary to achieve the objective of the exception. Rightsholders and beneficiaries may establish commonly accepted best practices regarding the implementation of the obligation and measures"
- contractual arrangements contrary to the exception are unenforceable (§28(1), §44(1))
remarks: "In terms of subject matter, the approach to the implementation of the CDSM Directive differs from the referencing scheme of the pre-existing E&Ls system, where there is one main exception applying to 'intellectual property' (meaning works, incl. computer programmes, but also sui generis right databases, phonograms, broadcasts and publications of previously unpublished works) and a reference to related rights (meaning press publications and performances). Here, the provision of §24 covers 'works and other protected subject matter'. In addition, according to §44(3), the provisions of §§ 24 to 43 apply to all works and other subject matter protected by national copyright law on or after the date of entry into force of the provisions of this Law. At the same time, §24 explicitly refers to different types of usage for different subject matter. In that, it does not refer to any use of computer programmes nor of press publications. While software is excluded from the scope of the EU exception, press publications must be covered. The legal uncertainty surrounding whether press publications fall within the scope of the exception warrants a lower score.<br /><br/>The exception is safeguarded against contractual override (§28(1), §44(1)). However, it does not appear to be protected against circumvention via TPMs. Since many pre-existing exceptions are safeguarded against TPMs under §14B(3) and (4), this is most likely an omission on the part of the legislator during the implementation of Article 7(2) of the CDSM Directive. This omission, however, also warrants a lower score for the exception."
