description: "This exception allows for specific acts of reproduction of a work carried out by publicly accessible libraries, educational institutions or museums or by archives and which are not intended, directly or indirectly, for economic or commercial gain." 
beneficiaries:
- publicly accessible libraries, educational institutions, museums and archives

- non-commercial
usage:
- reproduction (specific acts)
subjectmatter:
- works (incl. computer programmes)
- databases
- phonograms
- film fixations
- broadcasts
- publications of previously unpublished works
- performances
- press publications
compensation:
- no compensation required
attribution: 
- no attribution required
otherConditions:
- the application of the exception is subject to the exceptions and limitations provided for in articles 24 to 44 of the Law
remarks: "The Cypriot implementation of art. 5(2)(c) of the InfoSoc Directive is broad and closely follows the EU model provision. However, with the transposition of the CDSM Directive, an additional text was introduced in fine that appears to function as a form of conflict rule vis-à-vis the new exceptions implementing arts 3-8. Specifically, it provides that 'the application of the exception is subject to the exceptions and limitations laid down in Articles 24 to 44,' which suggests that the exceptions introduced pursuant to arts 3–8 of the CDSM Directive take precedence over the pre-existing provision.<br /><br/>In terms of subject matter, Cyprus' exceptions apply to 'intellectual property'. According to Section 3(1)(a) of the Law, the material scope of protection of 'intellectual property' includes works, incl. computer programmes, but also databases, phonograms, broadcasts and publications of previously unpublished works. Furthermore, Section 3(1)(c)(iii) of the Law states that the exceptions and limitations recognized in art. 3 and in art. 7 vis-a-vis 'intellectual property' also apply to related rights, in the absence of a contrary provision. According to Section 3(1)(b) the objects of related rights are press publications and performances."
