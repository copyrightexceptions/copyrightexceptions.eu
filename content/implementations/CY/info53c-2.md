---
title: "Section 7(2)(g), 2nd sentence, of the Copyright Law"
date: 2004
draft: false
weight: 53
exceptions:
- info53c-2
jurisdictions:
- CY
score: 3
description: "This exception allows for the use of works or other objects in the presentation of current affairs, to the extent justified by the informational purpose and provided that the source is indicated, including the name of the author, unless impossible." 
beneficiaries:
- any user
purposes: 
- informational purpose
- presentation of current affairs
usage:
- any use (within the presentation of current affairs)
subjectmatter:
- works (incl. computer programmes)
- databases
- phonograms
- film fixations
- broadcasts
- publications of previously unpublished works
- performances
- press publications
compensation:
- no compensation required
attribution: 
- the source, including the name of the author, must be indicated unless it is established that this is impossible
otherConditions: 
- use must be effected to the extent justified by the informational purpose
remarks: "Although Section 7(2)(g) is a literal transposition of art.5(3)(c) of the InfoSoc Directive, some informatory uses can also be found in Section 7(2)(a) of the Law. According to the latter, it is permitted to use, in a spirit of good faith, for the purpose of 'critical review or reference to current events, provided that, if such use is made publicly, it is accompanied by acknowledgment of the title and authorship of the work, except in the case where the work was incidentally included in a broadcast.'<br /><br/>In terms of subject matter, Cyprus' exceptions apply to 'intellectual property'. According to Section 3(1)(a) of the Law, the material scope of protection of 'intellectual property' includes works, incl. computer programmes, but also databases, phonograms, broadcasts and publications of previously unpublished works. Furthermore, Section 3(1)(c)(iii) of the Law states that the exceptions and limitations recognized in art. 3 and in art. 7 vis-a-vis 'intellectual property' also apply to related rights, in the absence of a contrary provision. According to Section 3(1)(b) the objects of related rights are press publications and performances."
