description: "There are two exceptions that may be viewed as implementing the EU Freedom of Panorama exception in Cyprus. According to Section 7(2)(b), the inclusion in a film or broadcast of any artistic work situated in a place where it may be viewed by the public is permitted. Section 7(2)(c) allows for the reproduction and distribution of copies of any artistic work permanently situated in a place where it can be viewed by the public."

remarks: "The two provisions cover different types of usage - inclusion in a film or broadcast, on the one hand, and reproduction and distribution of copies, on the other. It remains unclear whether the exceptions cover making available to the public or not. Although there are no further conditions for the permitted use, the uncertainty regarding the types of permitted use warrants a lower score.<br /><br/>In terms of subject matter, this specific exception only covers 'artistic works permanently situated in a place where they can be viewed by the public'. However, Cyprus' exceptions as a whole apply to 'intellectual property'. According to Section 3(1)(a) of the Law, the material scope of protection of 'intellectual property' includes works, incl. computer programmes, but also databases, phonograms, broadcasts and publications of previously unpublished works. Furthermore, Section 3(1)(c)(iii) of the Law states that the exceptions and limitations recognized in art. 3 and in art. 7 vis-a-vis 'intellectual property' also apply to related rights, in the absence of a contrary provision. According to Section 3(1)(b) the objects of related rights are press publications and performances."
