description: "This exception allows for temporary acts of reproduction which are transient or incidental reproductions, such as acts which enable browsing as well as acts of caching, including those which enable transmission systems to function efficiently, provided that the intermediary does not modify the information and does not interfere with the lawful use of technology, widely recognised and used by industry, to obtain data on the use of the information. A use is considered lawful where it is authorised by the rightholder or not restricted by law." 
beneficiaries:
- intermediaries (transmission between third parties in a network)
- any user (authorized use)

- other authorized use

- temporary acts of reproduction

- works (incl. computer programmes)

- phonograms

- broadcasts

- performances

compensation:
- no compensation required

otherConditions: 
- temporary acts of reproduction must be transient or incidental, such as acts which enable browsing and caching, as well as enable transmission systems to function efficiently
- intermediary must not modify the information and must not interfere with the lawful use of technology
- use is considered legal as long as it is permitted by the owner or is not restricted by law
remarks: "The Cypriot implementation of art. 5.1. of the InfoSoc Directive contains a sentence, according to which a use is considered lawful 'as long as it is permitted by the owner or is not restricted by law'. It is somewhat unclear if this condition refers to 'lawful use' as a purpose for the temporary reproduction, or to the exception as a whole. According to commentators, Section 7(5) must be read together with Section 2, which contains a legal definition of the term 'temporary acts of reproduction'. Still the uncertainty of whether an act of reproduction is only lawful when allowed by the rightsholder remains (see A Demetriades & N Epaminonda in ‘Cyprus’ (2019), B Lindner and T Shapiro (eds), Copyright in the Information Society, Elgar Intellectual Property Law and Practice, 257). This legal uncertainty warrants a low score for the exception.<br /><br/>In terms of subject matter, Cyprus' exceptions apply to 'intellectual property'. According to Section 3(1)(a) of the Law, the material scope of protection of 'intellectual property' includes works, incl. computer programmes, but also databases, phonograms, broadcasts and publications of previously unpublished works. Furthermore, Section 3(1)(c)(iii) of the Law states that the exceptions and limitations recognized in art. 3 and in art. 7 vis-a-vis 'intellectual property' also apply to related rights, in the absence of a contrary provision. According to Section 3(1)(b) the objects of related rights are press publications and performances."
