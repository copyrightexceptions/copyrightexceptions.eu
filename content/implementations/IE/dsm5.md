description: "Ireland has several exceptions dealing with educational uses. The implementation of Article 5 of the CDSM Directive took place through an amendment of Section 57 and the addition of Sections 57A to 57C in 2019 and 2021. These provisions deal with illustration for education, teaching or scientific research (§57), distance learning provided by educational establishments (§57A), and use by educational establishments of works available through the internet (§57B). In the case of the general provision of §57, permitted use is limited to not more than 5 per cent of a work in any calendar year. Section 57C contains a carve-out from all these exceptions whenever there is a licence applicable to the exemption concerned and the person making use of the work knew or ought to have been aware of the existence of the licensing scheme."

remarks: "Section 57A contains safeguards against override by contract and TPMs (only concerning distance learning provided by an educational establishment). Pursuant to §57A(5) 'any contractual provisions contrary to this section shall be unenforceable'. As per §57A(4), 'where a beneficiary of an exception or limitation under this section has legal access to the relevant protected work or subject matter, the relevant author or performer shall ensure that that beneficiary has the means of benefiting from that exception or limitation to the extent necessary to do so.' Nevertheless, the applicability of exceptions for illustration for education, teaching or scientific research (§57) and distance learning provided by educational establishments (§57A) is subject to the existence of licenses - not restricted to specific subject matter - of which the user is aware or should have been aware.<br /><br/>In Ireland, there is no overall distinction made between works of authorship and related rights. Certain provisions are stated to apply to some but not all rights holders - for exemple moral rights are granted only to authors of literary, dramatic, musical, artistic works and film. However in general the provisions relating to ownership, duration, permitted acts, dealings and so forth, apply in the absence of a specific exclusion to every 'work', with ‘work’ defined as a literary, dramatic, musical or artistic work, sound recording, film, broadcast, cable programme, typographical arrangement or a published edition, or an original database, and includes a computer programme. Performances and works protected by the database right are dealt with separately to the main scheme. (see Linda Scales in ‘Ireland’ (2019), B Lindner and T Shapiro (eds), Copyright in the Information Society, Elgar Intellectual Property Law and Practice, 471). The exceptions extend to recordings of performances as per Sections 225B and 225C CA. However, these provisions are also subject to licence availablility.<br /><br/>As for the application of the exception to press publications, it is regulated separately in §16 of in S.I. No 567 of 2021, according to which 'There shall be an exception from the rights provided for under Regulation 13 in order to allow the digital use of works or other subject matter for the sole purpose of illustration for teaching, to the extent justified by the non-commercial purpose to be achieved, on condition that such use takes place under the authority of an educational establishment, on its premises or at other venues, or through a secure electronic environment access to which is limited to an educational establishment’s teaching staff and to pupils or students enrolled in a study programme, in particular through appropriate authentication procedures including password-based authentication, and is accompanied by an indication of the source, including the author’s name, unless this turns out to be impossible' The exception appears not to directly extend to sui generis database rights, however educational uses are dealt with in Section 330 of the Act. It states that 'The database right in a database is not infringed by fair dealing with a substantial part of its contents by a lawful user of the database where that part is extracted for the purposes of illustration in the course of instruction or of preparation for instruction education or of preparation for education and where (i) the extraction is done by or on behalf of a person giving or receiving instruction education, and (ii) the source is indicated. For the purposes of this section the term 'lawful user' is said to include educational establishments."
