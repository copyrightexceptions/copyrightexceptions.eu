description: "This exception allows cultural heritage institutions to make available, for non-commercial purposes, out-of-commerce works or other subject matter that are permanently in their collections, where (i) no suficiently representative collective management organisation exists, (ii) a copyright owner has not excluded his or her work from the use, and (ii) works are only made available on non-commercial websites." 
beneficiaries:
- cultural heritage institutions
purposes: 
- non-commercial
usage:
- making available to the public (on non-commercial websites)
subjectmatter:
- works (incl. computer programs)
- press publications
- databases
compensation:
- no compensation required
attribution: 
- use must be accompanied by sufficient acknowledgment
otherConditions: 
- works used must be permanently in beneficiaries' collections
- no suficiently representative collective management organisation must exists
- a copyright owner must have not excluded his or her work from the use
remarks: "Section 58A(6) contains a legal definition for a work that may be considered to be 'permanently' in the collections of a cultural heritage institution. It is where the work is owned or permanently held by that institution, including as a result of a transfer of ownership or a licence agreement, legal deposit obligations or permanent custody arrangements.<br /><br/>In terms of subject matter, the text of 58A refers to 'out-of-commerce works or other subject matter' seemingly covering related rights as well. However, the exceptions appears not to specifically extend to recordings of performances as per §§ 225B and 225C CA. There are explicit provisions applying it to computer programs (§82A), sui generis database rights (§330A) and press publications (§17 of S.I. No 567 of 2021)."
