description: "This exception allows for the use of works and other protected subject matter - digitally or in digital form - by educational institutions for illustration for teaching in a volume justified for achieving its non-commercial purpose, to the extent that these actions are necessary for the purposes of illustration in teaching, under the control and responsibility of the educational institution - on its premises, on the premises of third parties or through a secure electronic environment accessible only to the relevant trainers and trainees, regardless of whether they are located in the same or another country. The exception does not apply to musical scores and works and other protected subject matter intended primarily for use in education, if the user can obtain the necessary permission after making reasonable efforts."

remarks: "This exception was introduced in parallel and partially overlaps with the implementation of art.5(2)(c) of the InfoSoc Directive (transposed in art. 24, para 1, item.3 of the Bulgarian Copyright law). There is a general collision norm in art.26l, according to which this exception 'shall not affect the application of' the pre-existing exception under art. 24 and 'shall not be limited by them'.<br /><br />The exception is imperative. According to the special provision of art.26l, para 2 (implementing art.7 of the CDSM Directive), 'any agreement that prevents or restricts the right to free use under articles 26g - 26i shall be null and void'. In addition, as per the more general norm of para 2 of art.23, any agreement that prevents or restricts the right to free use is null and void, unless otherwise provided by the law. Furthermore, under art.25a, para. 2, 'users who wish to benefit from the provisions of art.26g for works that they have a legal right to use, but are prevented by technical means of protection, may request the right holder to provide them with the relevant access to the extent justified by the purpose.This carve-out is further conditioned on users having made 'reasonable efforts' to obtain permission, an unorthodox formulation that may generate legal uncertainty and thus justifies a lower score for this national implementation.<br /><br />The provisions defines the scope of educational institutions, These are institutions in the system of pre-school and school education, institutions entered in the registers maintained under the Vocational Education and Training Actq and higher education institutions established in accordance with the Higher Education Act. This exception is applicable to all neighbouring rights.<br /><br />The exception covers all neighbouring rights. As of 2023, the respective referencing provisions for neighbouring rights include references to the entire art. 24."
