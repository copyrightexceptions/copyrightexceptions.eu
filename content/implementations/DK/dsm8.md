- DK

description: "This exception allows public archives, libraries, museums and film and sound heritage institutions to make copies and make available to the public out-of-commerce works which are a permanent part of the institution's collection, provided that the use is not for commercial purposes, unless there is a representative collective management organisation for the respective rightsholders or the rightsholder has prohibited the exploitation of the work." 
beneficiaries:
- public archives, libraries, museums and film and sound heritage institutions
purposes: 
- non-commercial
usage:
- reproduction
- making available to the public
subjectmatter:
- works
- performances
- phonograms
- film fixations
- broadcasts
- photographic pictures
- press publications
compensation:
- no compensation required
attribution: 
- no explicit attribution requirement
otherConditions: 
- works must be a permanent part of beneficiaries' collections
- the rightsholder must not have prohibited the exploitation of the work
- there must not be a representative collective management organisation for the respective rightsholders
- use is deemed to take place in Denmark if the public archive, library or museum or the public film or sound heritage institution is established in Denmark
- the Minister for Culture may lay down further rules on the requirements necessary to determine whether works are out-of-commerce
remarks: "Like in many other countries, Danish law does not appear to set out criteria for what constitutes 'sufficient' representativeness of CMOs for the purpose of applying this exception.<br /><br />The exception applies also to performances (§65(6)), sound recordings (§66(2)), film fixations (§67(2)), broadcasts (§69(3)), photographic pictures (§70(3)) and press publications (§ 69a(5) of the CA - added with the amendment of 3 June 2021)."
