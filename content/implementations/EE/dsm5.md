description: Estonia has introduced an exception aimed at implementing Article 5 DSM, which permits digital uses of works for illustration for teaching, under the responsibility of an educational establishments (§19(1)(&sup3;)). This exception applies to performances, phonograms, film fixations, broadcasts and press publications (§75(1)(6)) and to databases protected by sui generis right (§75&sup6;(5)).

remarks: The exception implementing Article 5 of the CDSM  Directive allows the reproduction of a lawfully published work in digital form and its communication to the public solely for the purpose of illustration to the extent justified by the purpose and on the condition that such use is carried out for non-commercial purposes, under the responsibility of the educational establishment on its premises or at other venues, or through a secure electronic environment accessible only by the educational establishment's pupils or students and teaching staff (§19(1)(3&sup2;)). This provision applies to all other protected subject matter (see §75(1)(6) and §75&sup6;(5)).<br /><br/> A contractual term that restricts the use of a work or other subject matter under this exception is void. The safeguard against technological protection measures, which implements Article 7(2) of the CDSM Directive, covers this exception. <br /><br/> There's a cross-border provision stating that the use of a protected in the manner specified under this exception shall be deemed to take place only in the Member State of the European Union or the state which has acceded to the Agreement on the European Economic Area where the relevant educational institution is located. <br /><br/> There are general exceptions allowing the use of works and protected subject matter for the purpose of illustration for teaching or scientific research, which overlap with the above-mentioned exception, but they do not have a similar cross-border provision, thus they are only considered in the context of the implementation of Article 5(3)(a) InfoSoc Directive.<br /><br/> The general education exception applicable to works was ammended with the implementation of Article 5 CDSM Directive, to mention that the "special rules" provided in the new exception for digital reproductions and communication to the public in educational establishments should be taken into consideration.<br /><br/>
