description: "This exception allows cultural heritage institutions to reproduce and make available to the public out-of-commerce objects in their collections, as well as translate and adapt computer programmes and databases, and extract from databases or from a substantial part thereof, for non-commercial purposes, unless there is a collective management organisation which would meet the conditions specified in clause 2 of subsection 2 of § 571 of this Act for exercising the rights with regard to the respective object of rights; or the rightholder has excluded the object from  use." 
beneficiaries:
- cultural heritage institutions
purposes: 
- non=commercial
usage:
- reproduction
- making available to the public
- translation and adaptation (computer programmes and databases)
- extraction (databases)
subjectmatter:
- works
- computer programmes
- performances
- phonograms
- film fixations
- broadcasts
- unpublished work
- literary criticism or scientific publication
- databases
- press publications
compensation:
- no compensation required
attribution: 
- the name of the author or other identifiable rightholder must be indicated, where possible
otherConditions: 
- works used must be in beneficiaries' collections
- the exception applies as long as there is no sufficiently representative CMO which would meet the conditions specified in §57<sup>1</sup>(2) of the Law with regard to the respective objects
- the rightholder may, at any time, easily and effectively, exclude the object of rights from the use, including after the beginning of the use by the cultural heritage institution
- use is deemed to take place only in the Member State of the EU or state which is a contracting party to the EEA Agreement, where the respective cultural heritage institution is established
remarks: "Estonia is one of the few countries with more detailed statutory requirements regarding the 'sufficient' representativeness of CMOs involved in out-of-commerce works extended collective licensing. Pursuant to §57<sup>1</sup>(2) of the Law, the collective management organisation entering into collective licence agreements with an extended effect 'must represent, under the mandates from the rightholder, a vast majority of the rightholders of the works or objects of related rights of the type concerned within the Republic of Estonia, and exercise the rights which are the subject of the licence'.<br /><br />The exception is extended to related rights with a general reference to 'other cases where the rights of authors of works are limited' pursuant to Chapter IV of this Act" in § 75 (6)."
