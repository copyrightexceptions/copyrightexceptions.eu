description: "This exception allows for the reproduction necessary for text and data mining of works, by any user, on the condition that they have lawful access to the work and the rightholder has not objected to the free use in advance and in an appropriate manner – in machine-readable form in the case of content made publicly available electronically." 
purposes: 
- text and data mining
usage:
- reproduction
- extraction
subjectmatter:
- works
- performances
- phonograms
- film fixations
- broadcasts
- press publications
- databases
compensation:
- no compensation required
attribution: 
- no attribution required
otherConditions: 
- lawful access
- copies necessary for text and data mining can be retained only for the period necessary for the purpose of text and data mining
- rightholders must have not objected to the free use in advance and in an appropriate manner
- as a general rule, works used must be disclosed (§33(1))
- as a general rule, exceptions are not overridable by contract (§33(4))
remarks: "Section 33 (1) of the law limits the applicability of exceptions and limitations to publicly available works only, while 33 (4) prohibits exclusion or restriction of free use by contract or unilateral legal declaration as null and void. The exception applies to all neighbouring rights. Pursuant to Section 83 of the Law, the consent of neighbouring rights holders is not required in cases where the author's consent is not required. The exception also extends to sui generis database rights under §84/D(2)."
