description: "Hungary has an exception covering digital uses within the framework of in-person digital education or remote education (§34(3)(b)). This exception also applies to performances, phonograms, broadcasts, film fixations, press publications (§83(2)). The exception is not subject to compensation."
beneficiaries:
- anyone in school education
purposes: 
- for the purpose of school education 
usage:
- any use
subjectmatter:
- any work (other than sheet music), performances, phonograms, broadcasts, film fixations, press publications
compensation:
- no compensation required (all exceptions)
attribution:
- the source and the name of the author must be indicated 
otherConditions: 
- quantity limitations: only allows the use of parts of a literary, musical or film work, to the extent justified by the purpose; works can however be used in their entirety when they are small entire works of such nature, as well as pictures of works of fine art, architecture, applied art and industrial design creations, and photographic works
- technological limitations: communication to the public must be made through a secured electronic environment, which is defined as a technical solution that prevents a work made available for retrieval from being carried out outside the scope of education and training 
- 3-step test: the use is permitted only to the extent that such use does not prejudice the normal exploitation of the work and does not unreasonably prejudice the legitimate interests of the author, and provided that it is fair and does not pursue a purpose incompatible with the permitted use (exception for “borrowing” from a work, exception for using recipient work, exception for reproduction, distribution and communication to the public)
remarks: "Hungary has a variety of education exceptions. The exception introduced with the aim of implementing Article 5 of the DSM Directive covers in-person uses in digital form on an electronic device at the place of school education and communication to the public through a secure electronic environment, provided that such uses are not made on a commercial basis. The exception does not apply to sheet music (§34(3b)), even if no licences are available in the market for such a category of works, which contradicts article 5(2) of the DSM Directive. The uses made under this exception shall be deemed to take place in the Member State in which the educational establishment is established (§34(3a)). <br /> There's a provision stating that, in the cases where the law allows the use of a copyrighted work without the authorization of the author, no authorization is required from the holders of neighbouring rights, but does not apply to databases protected by sui generis right (§83(2)).<br /> The law states that the exclusion or restriction of free use by contract or unilateral legal declaration is null and void. The safeguard against technological protection measures, which implements Article 7(2) of the CDSM Directive, covers these exceptions. <br /> There’s an exception which allows a significant part of the content of the databases protected by the sui generis right to be copied for the purpose of school education or scientific research, but it does not contain a cross-border provision, so it has not been deemed as implementing Article 5 of the DSM Directive."
