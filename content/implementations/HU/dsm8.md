description: "This exception allows for the reproduction and communication to the public of a work that is out of commercial circulation, nor for the adaptation and distribution of software, if the use is aimed at making available, provided that the work is a permanent part of the collection of the cultural heritage institutions; the communication to the public is carried out on a website maintained for non-commercial purposes, and; the collective management organisation representing the exclusive right to exercise the rights in respect of the type of work to be used does not have the authorisation and the applicable fee schedule for exercising the rights. Also, The author or the rightholder of related rights may object to the use at any time, even after the use has begun."
beneficiaries:
- cultural heritage institutions (implied)
purposes: 
usage:
- reproduction
- adaptation and distribution (of software)
- communication to the public (on a website maintained for non-commercial purposes)
subjectmatter:
- works (incl.software)
- performances
- phonograms
- film fixations
- broadcasts
- press publications
compensation:
- no compensation required
attribution: 
- the name of the author or the right holder of related rights must be indicated during use, if possible
otherConditions: 
- works used must be a permanent part of the beneficiaries' collections
- there must not be a collective management organisation representing the exclusive right to exercise the rights in respect of the type of work to be used does not have the authorisation and the applicable fee schedule for exercising the rights
- the author or the rightholder of related rights must not have objected to the use; use must be terminated within a reasonable time after the objection and the author or the rightholder of related rights is not be entitled to remuneration for uses made before objection
- the free use is deemed to be use in the Member State in which the cultural heritage institution is established
remarks: "Section 33 (1) of the law limits the applicability of exceptions and limitations to publicly available works only, while 33 (4) prohibits exclusion or restriction of free use by contract or unilateral legal declaration as null and void. The exception applies to all neighbouring rights. Pursuant to Section 83 of the Law, the consent of neighbouring rights holders is not required in cases where the author's consent is not required. The exception does not appear to extend to sui generis database rights under ยง 84/D. Although the exception is broadly formulated, the absence of any reference to database rights limits its material scope compared with the provision in the Directive."
