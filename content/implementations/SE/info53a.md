description: "Educational uses are covered by Articles 13, 14 and 15 of the Swedish Copyright Act. Article 13 allows teachers and students at educational institutions to reproduce works for illustrative purposes in connection with teaching. In distance learning, works may only be reproduced in a secure electronic environment. The exception does not apply to uses that can be made on the basis of a contractual licence, if such is readily available on the market. Article 14 allows teachers and students to make recordings of their own performances of works for teaching purposes. Finally, Article 15 allows producers of collections of works compiled from works by a large number of authors for use in teaching, to reproduce minor parts of literary and musical works and such works of small scope, if five years have passed after the year in which the works were published. Works of art may be reproduced in connection with the text, if five years have passed after the year in which the work was published. The latter exception is remunerated, does not apply to works that have been created for use in teaching and does not cover the production of collections for commercial purposes."

remarks: "Sweden has a collection of educational exceptions that are rather narrow in scope. The 'illustration for teaching' exception only provides for the reproduction of works, and only whenever there is no contractual licence readily available on the market for the respective use. Formally, the exception is not overridable by contract and by technical protection measures (TPM) under art. 52f, para 2 of the Law, according to which 'Where a technological measure prevents such use, a court may, on application by an authorised user, order the author or his rightholder to enable the user to use the work in the manner specified in the relevant provision'. It extends to the rights of performers (art. 45(3) of the Law), photographers (art. 49a(4) of the Law), phonograms and film producers (art. 46(3) of the Law), broadcasting organisations (art. 48(3) of the Law), and database makers (art. 49(3) of the Law), but does not cover the rights of press publishers (art. 48b(4) of the Law).<br /><br/>“As for the exceptions provided for in arts. 14 and 15, they appear to be extremely limited in scope, are not protected against contractual override, and do not extend to any neighbouring rights.<br /><br/>Art. 11(2) requires that when a work is used publicly under an exception, the source must be, as a general rule, stated to the extent and in the manner required by proper usage. It also requires that the work must not be altered more than necessary for the intended use."
