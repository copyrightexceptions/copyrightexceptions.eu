description: "This exception allows for the use of published works by making digital copies, by public performance, or by communication to the public, for illustration for teaching and research, if such use does not result in direct or indirect financial gain."
beneficiaries:
- any user
purposes: 
- non-commercial 
usage:
- reproduction
- public performance
- communication to the public
subjectmatter:
- works
- performances
- phonograms
- film fixations
- broadcasts
- press publications 
compensation:
- no compensation required
attribution: 
-  the name of the author or his pseudonym, the title of the work and the source to be stated, if possible given the nature of the work and the manner in which the work is used
otherConditions: 
remarks: "At the time of the implementation of the CDSM Directive, Slovakia already had a very broad teaching and research exception, almost identical to the model clause in art. 5(3)(a) of the InfoSoc Directive. The specificities concerning digital teaching (use under the responsibility of the institution and the country-of-origin mechanism) were added in separate provisions in 2022.<br /><br/>Pursuant to § 44(4), a school means a school, educational facility, university, educational institution pursuant to a special regulation and childcare facility for children under three years of age.<br /><br/>With the implementation of the CDSM Directive, Slovakia introduced a general prohibition of contractual override of exceptions in a new § 35(4). As for technical protection measures, pursuant to § 60(4), the prohibition on the circumvention of TPMs does not apply to the use of a work under § 44, to the extent necessary to exercise the relevant exception or limitation.<br /><br/>The exception applies to performers' (§ 103), phonogram producers' (§113), audiovisual producers' (§121) and broadcasters' (§127.1), and press publishers' rights (§129e) .<br /><br/>§ 35 contains a general obligation for the name of the author or his pseudonym, the title of the work and the source to be stated, if possible given the nature of the work and the manner in which the work is used."
