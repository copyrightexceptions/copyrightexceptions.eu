title: "Section 44(2) of the Copyright Act"
date: 2022-03-25
draft: false
weight: 12
exceptions:
- dsm5
jurisdictions:
- SK
score: 2
description: "This exception allows for the use of published works by making digital copies, by public performance, or by communication to the public for the purpose of illustration in teaching at a school, under the responsibility of the school or through the school’s secure electronic network, for a non-commercial purpose." 
beneficiaries:
- any person (at a school, under the responsibility of the school or through the school’s secure electronic network)
purposes: 
- non-commercial 
usage:
- reproduction
- public performance
- communication to the public
subjectmatter:
- works
- performances
- phonograms
- film fixations
- broadcasts
- press publications 
compensation:
- no compensation required
attribution: 
- the name of the author or their pseudonym, if it is not an anonymous work, or the name of the person under whose name, business name or title the work is presented to the public, as well as the title of the work and the source must be stated, if possible given the nature of the work and the manner in which the work is used
otherConditions: 
- use must occur at a school, under the responsibility of the school or through the school’s secure electronic network
- use is deemed to occur in the territory of the state in which the school is established
remarks: "Pursuant to § 44(4), a school means a school, educational facility, university, educational institution pursuant to a special regulation and childcare facility for children under three years of age.<br /><br/>With the implementation of the CDSM Directive, Slovakia introduced a general prohibition of contractual override of exceptions in a new § 35(4). As for technical protection measures, pursuant to § 60(4), the prohibition on the circumvention of TPMs does not apply to the use of a work under § 44, to the extent necessary to exercise the relevant exception or limitation.<br /><br/>The exception applies to performers' (§ 103), phonogram producers' (§113), audiovisual producers' (§121) and broadcasters' (§127.1), and press publishers' rights (§129e). There is no reference to the sui generis database right.<br /><br/>§ 35 contains a general obligation for the name of the author or his pseudonym, the title of the work and the source to be stated, if possible given the nature of the work and the manner in which the work is used."
