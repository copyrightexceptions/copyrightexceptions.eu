title: "Section 51c of the Copyright Act"
date: 2022-03-25
draft: false
weight: 11
exceptions:
- dsm4
jurisdictions:
- SK
score: 2
description: "Any person can use a work by making a copy for data mining, unless such use is expressly reserved." 
beneficiaries:
- any user
purposes: 
- data mining
usage:
- reproduction
subjectmatter:
- works
- performances
- phonograms
- film fixations
- broadcasts
- press publications
- databases 
compensation:
- no compensation required
attribution: 
- no attribution required
otherConditions: 
- rights of use must not be 'expressly reserved'
- "storage and reuse: copies obtained may be kept for as long as is necessary for the purposes of data extraction"
remarks: "With the implementation of the CDSM Directive, Slovakia introduced a general prohibition of contractual override of exceptions in a new § 35(4). The exception under § 51c is one of the only two excepions in the law that are not safeguarded against override. As for technical protection measures, pursuant to § 60(4), the prohibition on the circumvention of TPMs does not apply to the use of a work under § 51c, to the extent necessary to exercise the relevant exception or limitation.<br /><br/>The exception applies to performers' (§ 103), phonogram producers' (§113), audiovisual producers' (§121) and broadcasters' (§127.1), press publishers' (§129e) and sui generis database rights (§138.4.e).<br /><br/>A potential shortcoming of the Slovak implementation of art. 4 of the CDSM Directive is that it appears not to establish any requirements for the reservation of rights (opt-out). It also appears not to include an explicit requirement for access to be lawful."
