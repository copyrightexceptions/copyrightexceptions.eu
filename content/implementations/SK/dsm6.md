title: "Section 49a of the Copyright Act"
date: 2022-03-25
draft: false
weight: 13
exceptions:
- dsm6
jurisdictions:
- SK
score: 3
description: "This exception allows libraries, archives, museums or legal depositories pursuant to a special regulation, to use works permanently deposited in a library, archive, museum or a legal depository, by making a copy for the purpose of preserving the work to the extent necessary for this purpose." 
beneficiaries:
- libraries, archives, museums, legal depositories
purposes: 
- preservation
usage:
- reproduction
subjectmatter:
- works
- performances
- phonograms
- film fixations
- broadcasts
- press publications
- databases
compensation:
- no compensation required
attribution: 
- the name of the author or their pseudonym, if it is not an anonymous work, or the name of the person under whose name, business name or title the work is presented to the public, as well as the title of the work and the source must be stated, if possible given the nature of the work and the manner in which the work is used
- otherConditions: 
-  works used must be permanently deposited in a library, archive, museum or a legal depository
-  use must be effected to the extent necessary for the purpose of preservation
remarks: "Slovakia implemented art.6 of the CDSM Directive in parallel with the existing implementation of art. 5(2)(c) of the InfoSoc Directive in § 49.<br /><br />With the implementation of the CDSM Directive, Slovakia introduced a general prohibition of contractual override of exceptions in a new § 35(4). As for technical protection measures, pursuant to § 60(4), the prohibition on the circumvention of TPMs does not apply to the use of a work under § 49a, to the extent necessary to exercise the relevant exception or limitation.<br /><br />The exception applies to performers' (§ 103), phonogram producers' (§113), audiovisual producers' (§121) and broadcasters' (§127.1), press publishers' (§129e) and sui generis database rights (§138(4)).<br /><br />§ 35 contains a general obligation for the name of the author or his pseudonym, the title of the work and the source to be stated, if possible given the nature of the work and the manner in which the work is used."
