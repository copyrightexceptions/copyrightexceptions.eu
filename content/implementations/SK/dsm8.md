title: "Section 51a of the Copyright Act"
date: 2022-03-25
draft: false
weight: 14
exceptions:
- dsm8
jurisdictions:
- SK
score: 3
description: "This exception allows libraries, archives, museums or legal depositories pursuant to a special regulation, to use out-of-commerce works permanently stored with the beneficiaries, by means of reproduction, communication to the public, extraction and reuse, for non-commercial purposes, if such use is not expressly reserved, and there is no representative collective rights management organisation for such use." 
beneficiaries:
- libraries, archives, museums, legal depositories
purposes: 
- non-commercial
usage:
- reproduction
- communication to the public
- processing, extraction and reuse (of databases)
- public dissemination and processing (of computer programmes)
subjectmatter:
- works
- performances
- phonograms
- film fixations
- broadcasts
- press publications
compensation:
- no compensation required
attribution: 
- the name of the author or their pseudonym, if it is not an anonymous work, or the name of the person under whose name, business name or title the work is presented to the public, as well as the title of the work and the source must be stated, if possible given the nature of the work and the manner in which the work is used
otherConditions: 
- works used must be permanently deposited in a library, archive, museum or a legal depository
- there must be no collective management organisation fulfilling the conditions under §79(1) of the law (a CMO eligible for ECL)
- use must not have been reserved
- use is deemed to take place in the territory of the State where the library, archive, museum or legal depository is established
remarks: "While §51a contains the fall-back exception as per art.8(2) of the CDSM Directive, the legal definition of an out-of-commerce work, together with the rest of the OOCW regime is regulated in §12 of the Copyright Law.<br /><br/>With the implementation of the CDSM Directive, Slovakia introduced a general prohibition of contractual override of exceptions in a new § 35(4). The exception applies to performers' (§ 103), phonogram producers' (§113), audiovisual producers' (§121) and broadcasters' (§127.1), and press publishers' rights (§129e).<br /><br/>§ 35 contains a general obligation for the name of the author or his pseudonym, the title of the work and the source to be stated, if possible given the nature of the work and the manner in which the work is used."
