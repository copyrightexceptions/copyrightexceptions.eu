description: "Slovakia has two partially overlapping preservation exceptions. Section 49 implements art. 5(2)(c) of the InfoSoc Directive and allows libraries, archives, museums or schools to use works deposited therein by making a copy for the purpose of substituting, archiving or securing of the original of the work or its copy against loss, destruction or damage. Section 49a is added in 2022 to implement art.6 of the CDSM Directive and allows libraries, archives, museums or legal depositories pursuant to a special regulation, to use works permanently deposited in a library, archive, museum or a legal depository, by making a copy for the purpose of preserving the work to the extent necessary for this purpose."

remarks: "Both exceptions apply to performers' (§103), phonogram producers' (§113), audiovisual producers' (§121), broadcasters' (§127.1) and press publishers' rights (§129e). Only §49a extends to sui generis database rights (§138(4)).<br /><br />With the implementation of the CDSM Directive, Slovakia introduced a general prohibition of contractual override of exceptions in a new § 35(4). As for technical protection measures, pursuant to § 60(4), the prohibition on the circumvention of TPMs does not apply to the use of a work under both §§ 49 and 49a, to the extent necessary to exercise the relevant exception or limitation.<br /><br />According to academia, beneficieries in the case of §49 can copy works from other institutions' collections. (see Olsovsky, S. and Jost, J. (2019) Slovak Republic. In: Lindner, B. and Shapiro T. (eds.) Copyright in the Information Society, p. 829).<br /><br />Pursuant to §35(2) of the Copyright Act, the name of the author or his pseudonym, if it is not an anonymous work, or the name of the person under whose name, trade name or title the work is presented to the public, as well as the work title and source, must be stated in all cases under §38 and §§ 40 to 57, if this is possible depending on the nature of the work and the manner of use of the work."
